Wagyu

Description

Wagyu is a type of beef steak that is rich in marbling, due to the way the cow is treated before harvested. Its fat content and distribution are the reason for the exquisite taste it is known for. Although these cuts are normally imported from Japan, there are American versions of the cut as well. However, they are both well above the normal price for an average grade of steak.

Ingredients

  • Wagyu cut of choice
  • Salt of choice
  • Cast iron skillet

Steps

  1. Thaw out wagyu steak to room temperature. The meat should be soft when touched
  2. Once meat is at room temperature, salt both sides liberally with the salt of your choice. Try not to oversalt, find the perfect amount.
  3. When finished salting, heat the cooking surface to at least 350 degrees Fahrenheit. Once it reaches cooking temperature, place steak on surface.
  4. Cook to desired likeness. Time will vary depending on thickness of the cut and heat of the surface. If unsure, try to at least get to an internal temperature of 145-180 degrees Fahrenheit, with even coloring on each side of the steak.
  5. Cut, serve, enjoy.
